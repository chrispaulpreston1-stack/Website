Key Findings at a Glance

40.9%

Major residential appeals allowed

Nearly half of all refused major schemes are overturned on appeal. Double the 23.1% rate for minor residential. The trend is worsening — 5-year rate is higher than the 10-year average.

£66m–£166m

Taxpayer costs burden

Estimated direct costs to LPAs from 2,670 allowed appeals where costs were awarded. Includes 60 public inquiries, 223 hearings, 2,327 written representations.

3,046

Green Belt appeals allowed

The largest single constraint category. Each represents a case where the LPA refused and an inspector found the evidence insufficient to justify refusal.

749

Committee decisions identified

From 30,000 decision letters (91.8% coverage). 87 explicitly against officer recommendation. Committee refusals account for 89.7% of identifiable decision routes in overturned appeals.

Reg 5(2)

Proposed amendment

Four specific, measurable gateway test triggers to replace the subjective "significant planning matters" criterion. Drafted for direct insertion into the Regulations.

1,900/yr

Homes delivered faster

If the reform reduces the major residential overturn rate to 30%, approximately 1,900 additional homes per year would be delivered without appeal delay.

Methodology & Data Sources

Primary source: PINS Casework Database downloaded from GOV.UK on 28 March 2026 under Open Government Licence. 188,933 total records parsed, of which 114,700 are planning appeals decided between 2015 and 2025. Zero hallucination — every figure independently verifiable from the source dataset.

Decision letter classification: 30,000 decision letters retrieved from the PINS Appeals Casework Portal (91.8% of all allowed appeals). Text extracted and classified by decision route. 749 committee decisions identified, 87 against officer recommendation. The largest systematic analysis of committee vs delegated planning decisions from primary PINS data.

Case studies: 500 verified case studies mined from 8,063 qualifying appeals using a scoring framework prioritising major residential schemes, costs awards, constrained sites, and inquiry-level proceedings.

Supplementary sources: 86 retrospective case studies across 59 English LPAs (Site Intelligence platform validation), Lichfields ("Refused for Good Reason?", 2018), PAS Modernising Planning Committees National Survey (December 2024), PINS Statistical Release (July 2025).
