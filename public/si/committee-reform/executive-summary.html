30,000 decision letters analysed. 749 committee refusals identified. 87 against officer recommendation — inspector sided with the officer every time.

The largest systematic analysis of committee vs delegated planning decisions ever conducted from primary PINS data. 91.8% coverage of all allowed appeals. Committee refusals account for 89.7% of identifiable decision routes among overturned appeals. Officer overrides up 164% since 2021. Committee determinations up 28%. The problem is accelerating — the reform is urgent.

Five Recommendations (mapped to specific clauses)

(i) Amend Reg 5 + Guidance Para 19: Replace subjective gateway criteria with four testable triggers: material departure, unresolved statutory consultee objection, 50+ representations, novel planning issue.
(ii) New para after Guidance Para 19: Require standardised evidence-based decision support for all gateway test decisions.
(iii) New para after Guidance Para 14: Mandate training for the nominated member role. PAS: 45% lack understanding after training. Draft guidance is silent on this.
(iv) New sub-para to Guidance Para 20: Address out-of-date plans. 78% of plans out of date. Where tilted balance (NPPF 11(d)) engaged, gateway threshold must be higher.
(v) Elevate Guidance Para 27 into Regulations: Make transparency reporting a statutory duty. Mandate annual reporting of delegation rates, appeal outcomes, costs awards.
