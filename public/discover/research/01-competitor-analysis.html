Competitor Data Source Analysis

How Site Intelligence's data spine compares to Landmark, Groundsure, Searchland, LandTech, and every other competitor in the market.

The Key Insight

Every competitor in the environmental search and site sourcing market pulls from the same government open data spine — Environment Agency, BGS, Historic England, Natural England, ONS, HM Land Registry, and Ordnance Survey. Differentiation comes from three things: (a) proprietary models or exclusive data partnerships, (b) the presentation and analysis layer, and (c) speed of integration. Site Intelligence's edge is full AI-automated analysis across all sources simultaneously — no other competitor does that.

Argyll buys Landmark/Envirocheck data and adds a qualified environmental consultant review on top. They're a reseller with a human analysis layer — the only provider in this list that offers fully consultant-led interpretation of contaminated land and flood risk data.

Their Unique Edge

The only provider offering a fully consultant-reviewed opinion on the data. This appeals to risk-averse buyers (solicitors, mortgage lenders) who want a named professional's assessment. However, it's slow (5-10 days) and expensive. SI's AI analysis provides a similar interpretive layer at a fraction of the time and cost.

What Competitors Have That SI Doesn't (Yet)

An honest assessment of data gaps. These are datasets that require commercial licenses or exclusive partnerships — they cannot be replicated with government open data alone.

Coal Authority CON29M Mining Search
Who has it: Groundsure, Landmark, FCI — Licensed dataset, ~£40/search

Required for properties in coal mining areas. SI should flag coal areas and recommend a separate CON29M search.

170+ Years Historical Mapping Archive
Who has it: Landmark (proprietary "Legend" database), Groundsure (OS licensed) — Commercially licensed raster data

Full historical map regression from 1840s onwards. SI uses historic map references and descriptive analysis but does not have the full raster archive for visual map regression.

Proprietary Flood Modelling
Who has it: Groundsure (Ambiental), FCI (JBA) — Commercial 5m resolution models

Higher-resolution flood modelling than EA free data. SI uses authoritative EA data which is the official government position, but commercial models offer more granular analysis.

Subsidence Claims Data
Who has it: FCI (Property Assure exclusive) — Insurance claims data, not publicly available

Historical insurance claims for subsidence. This is genuinely exclusive to FCI and cannot be replicated.

Rightmove / Zoopla Live Listings
Who has it: Searchland, Nimbus, LandTech (Hometrack) — Commercial API subscriptions

Live property listings and valuation comparables. Useful for site sourcing but not core to SI's environmental/planning analysis mission.

BNG Biodiversity Net Gain Data
Who has it: Searchland (GAIA partnership) — Emerging commercial dataset

Biodiversity net gain unit calculations. An emerging requirement — SI could partner or build this capability as the market matures.

Forward Climate Modelling
Who has it: Groundsure (ClimateIndex) — Proprietary climate scenario projection

Projects future flood, subsidence, and coastal erosion scenarios under climate change. A growing differentiator as climate risk becomes more prominent in planning.
