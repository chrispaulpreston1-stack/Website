◆ The Developer's View

Falconers is a Grade II listed Kentish Wealden manor house set in approximately 7.2 acres on Moult Hill, Salcombe — one of England's most exclusive coastal addresses. Originally constructed for the 1925 British Empire Exhibition at Wembley in hand-crafted English oak, it was subsequently dismantled and reassembled on this elevated plot above North Sands beach.

From a development perspective, this site presents a fascinating tension: extraordinary land value trapped behind extraordinary constraints. The combination of Grade II listing, South Devon National Landscape (AONB), likely Conservation Area inclusion, principal residence restrictions, and a heritage-sensitive neighbourhood means any development must be surgically precise in both design and planning strategy.

That said, there are realistic opportunities — and the research reveals that comparable development on Moult Hill has been approved. The question is not whether anything can be done, but what can be done well.

◆ Heritage Significance

Falconers (Historic England List Entry 1289162) is a Grade II listed building of considerable architectural interest. Built in 1925 by the Federated Home Grown Timber Merchants Association for the British Empire Exhibition at Wembley Park, it demonstrates the traditional Kentish Wealden vernacular — a medieval hall-house form using English oak with exposed beams, king-post trusses, and a central open hall.

After the exhibition, the building was carefully dismantled and transported to Moult Hill, Salcombe, where it was reassembled. Features of special interest include:

  • Full-height great hall with central timber truss and galleried landing
  • Stained glass windows
  • Studded oak entrance door
  • Original oak structural frame throughout
  • Grand fireplaces

The west wing has been extended (indicating at least one prior LBC approval), demonstrating that sensitive alteration has been accepted. The gardens are believed to reflect the influence of Gertrude Jekyll, with naturalistic planting and herbaceous borders, which may constitute a non-designated heritage asset in their own right.

◆ Constraint Mapping

Falconers sits within a layered constraint environment. Every designation adds a policy test that any development proposal must satisfy. The key is understanding which constraints are absolute barriers and which can be navigated with the right approach.

Grade II Listed Building
Listed Building Consent required for ANY works affecting character (internal or external). Curtilage structures pre-1948 also listed. Criminal offence to carry out unauthorised works. Permitted development severely curtailed.
South Devon National Landscape (AONB)
Highest status of landscape protection. Great weight to conserving and enhancing. Major development refused unless exceptional circumstances. CROW Act 2000 s.89 duty + LURA 2023 s.245 duty to further statutory purposes.
Principal Residence Restriction (SALC H3)
All new dwellings must be occupied as principal residence, secured by S106 in perpetuity. Upheld by Planning Inspector June 2025. No new second homes or unrestricted market housing.
Conservation Area (Check Required)
Salcombe Conservation Area designated 1973, extended 1985/1989/2010. Moult Hill's elevated Victorian villas noted in the Conservation Area Appraisal. Boundary check needed to confirm inclusion. Demolition requires consent.
Heritage Cluster Setting
Four listed buildings on Moult Hill including Grade II* The Moult. Any development requires assessment of impact on setting of all heritage assets (GPA3). Cumulative impact a material consideration.
South Hams SAC (Greater Horseshoe Bats)
1,000+ adult greater horseshoe bats (31% UK population). Sustenance Zones and Landscape Connectivity Zones trigger Habitats Regulations Assessment. Lighting impacts critical. Survey evidence required.
Tree Preservation Orders (Likely)
TPOs confirmed on Moult Hill (966 W1, 676 W1). Given mature woodland character and lime-tree-lined drive, TPOs highly likely on or adjacent to site. Works without consent is criminal offence.
NPPF Footnote 7 — Tilted Balance Displaced
AONB + listed building designations mean the presumption in favour of sustainable development (tilted balance) does not apply. Housing supply arguments cannot override heritage/landscape protection.
Flood Risk — Zone 1
Elevated hillside site. Less than 0.1% annual probability of river/sea flooding. Low constraint. Surface water drainage may still require assessment.
Salcombe-Kingsbridge Estuary SSSI
SSSI designation covers the estuary below, not the hill. Proximity is a material consideration but not a direct constraint on the site itself.
No CIL Liability
South Hams has not adopted CIL. S106 is used exclusively for developer contributions. For householder/minor development, unlikely to trigger S106 beyond the principal residence requirement.
Article 2(3) Land
Listed building within AONB. PD rights severely restricted: no side extensions, rear limited to 4m, no Class Q barn conversions. Most works require express planning permission.

Five realistic development scenarios assessed against the constraint and policy framework. Each scenario is rated for feasibility and includes the planning route, key risks, and indicative value creation.

A. Outbuilding Conversion & Annexe
HIGH FEASIBILITY

The Proposition

Convert existing detached outbuildings (garage, barn, ancillary structures) into habitable ancillary accommodation — a self-contained annexe tied to the main house by condition. Alternatively, replace an existing outbuilding with a new annexe of equivalent or reduced footprint.

Planning Route

  • NPPF 84(c) Re-use of redundant or disused buildings enhancing the immediate setting
  • Householder application (HHO) if ancillary to main dwelling
  • Listed Building Consent required if within the curtilage (pre-1948 structures are curtilage listed)
  • If outbuildings post-date 1948, they are not curtilage listed — LBC may only be needed for setting impact

Precedent

Cedar House, Moult Hill (3164/23/FUL) — detached annexe approved 2023. This is a direct, recent, same-street precedent. The white-horses garage-with-office also establishes the principle.

Key Risks

  • Structural condition of existing outbuildings may not support conversion without substantial rebuild
  • Scale and design must be subordinate to the principal building and not harm its setting
  • If the annexe becomes a separate dwelling in practice, enforcement risk (see Moult Hill Barn precedent)
  • Bat survey likely required given South Hams SAC

Indicative Value

A well-executed 2-3 bedroom annexe in this location could add £400,000–£750,000 to the overall property value as ancillary accommodation. As a separate dwelling (if achievable), potentially £1m+.

B. Sensitive Extension of the Listed Building
HIGH FEASIBILITY

The Proposition

Build on the precedent of the existing west-wing extension. A carefully designed, subordinate addition — perhaps a garden room, orangery, or pool house — that defers to the Wealden character while providing contemporary amenity.

Planning Route

  • Full planning permission + Listed Building Consent
  • Heritage Impact Assessment demonstrating less than substantial harm, outweighed by public benefits
  • NPPF 215 Less-than-substantial-harm test: balance against securing optimum viable use
  • Design must be "of the highest quality" — contemporary contrast or careful contextual response both viable approaches

Precedent

The existing west-wing extension at Falconers itself proves the principle. LBC has previously been granted for an extension to this specific building.

Key Risks

  • Historic England may object if the extension affects the great hall, principal elevations, or stained glass
  • Conservation officer will require detailed justification and reversibility assessment
  • AONB landscape impact if the extension increases the building's visual footprint when viewed from distance

Indicative Value

A high-specification extension (pool house, orangery, or additional reception space) could add £200,000–£500,000 while enhancing the property's market appeal at the £5m+ level.

C. Subdivision into Two Dwellings
MODERATE FEASIBILITY

The Proposition

Subdivide the 8-bedroom listed building into two self-contained dwellings — retaining the principal house (5-6 beds) and creating a secondary dwelling (2-3 beds) from a wing, potentially the west wing extension. Each with its own access and curtilage.

Planning Route

  • NPPF 84(d) Explicitly permits subdivision of existing residential buildings in the countryside
  • Full planning permission + Listed Building Consent
  • The secondary dwelling would require a SALC H3 principal residence S106
  • Heritage argument: subdivision may secure the building's long-term future by making it more viable as two smaller, more manageable residences rather than one unwieldy 8-bedroom house

The Case For

  • NPPF §84(d) provides explicit policy support — this is not a "new dwelling in the countryside" issue
  • 8 bedrooms is arguably beyond optimal viable use for a single household
  • Two principal-residence dwellings in Salcombe directly addresses the local housing imbalance (57% second homes)
  • Revenue from the second unit helps fund the ongoing maintenance of the Grade II listed fabric

Key Risks

  • LBC is the critical hurdle — subdivision must not harm the significance of the building (e.g., the great hall must remain intact and experienced as a single space)
  • Conservation officer may resist the loss of the "reading" of the building as a single grand residence
  • Parking, access, and bin storage for two dwellings in a heritage-sensitive setting
  • The Town Council's sensitivity to any intensification on Moult Hill

Indicative Value

Two dwellings could collectively be worth more than the single house. A 5-bed principal house might achieve £3.5–4.5m and a 2-3 bed secondary £1.5–2m, total £5–6.5m. Against a £4.75m guide price, the upside here is genuine — even a conservative £5m combined value covers the purchase with margin for works.

D. Paddock Development — Separate Title Opportunity (DN263903)
MODERATE–HIGH FEASIBILITY

The Proposition

The 2.98-acre paddock (DN263903) is held under a separate freehold title to the west/northwest of the main house. This is not curtilage garden — it is separately registered land that could potentially be developed or sold independently. A single architect-designed dwelling under NPPF §84(e), or a small enabling development scheme, becomes significantly more viable when the land is not directly tied to the listing.

Why the Separate Title Changes Everything

  • Curtilage argument weakened: A separate registered title with its own ownership history is strong evidence that the paddock is not within the curtilage of the listed building. Curtilage listing only applies to structures within the curtilage — if the paddock falls outside, development faces setting-impact assessment only, not direct LBC requirements.
  • Independent disposal: The paddock could be sold as a standalone development opportunity without affecting the listed house. A 2.98-acre plot on Moult Hill, Salcombe would command significant interest even without planning permission.
  • Reduced heritage friction: Development on the paddock must still consider impact on the setting of Falconers and nearby listed buildings, but this is the "less than substantial harm" test (NPPF §215) rather than the more onerous direct-harm test. Setting impact can be mitigated through landscaping, positioning, and design.
  • Functional land, not designed landscape: The paddock contains a helipad and grazing — it is not the Gertrude Jekyll-influenced formal gardens. Its contribution to the significance of the listed building is limited.

Planning Routes

  • NPPF 84(e) Exceptional quality design — "truly outstanding, reflecting the highest standards in architecture." Design review panel likely required. This bar is high but Salcombe values justify the architectural spend.
  • NPPF 84(b) Optimal viable use of a heritage asset / enabling development — if structured as cross-subsidy for Falconers restoration.
  • Full planning application (not householder) as the paddock is a separate land parcel
  • Principal residence restriction via S106 (SALC H3)
  • LVIA essential for AONB impact assessment
  • Bat survey and potential HRA for South Hams SAC

Key Risks

  • Town Council sensitivity — "first new building for over 50 years on Moult Hill"
  • AONB landscape impact from elevated site visible from distance
  • The §84(e) bar is genuinely high — requires award-calibre architecture
  • Greater horseshoe bat habitat must be assessed
  • Access arrangements — does the paddock have independent access or does it rely on a right of way through DN170405?

Indicative Value

An exceptional 4-5 bedroom dwelling on 2.98 acres of Moult Hill could achieve £2.5–4.0m. Build costs of £750k–1.2m for a 3,000 sq ft exceptional design. Residual land/profit: £1.5–2.5m. Even without planning, the paddock as a standalone plot with potential could be worth £500k–1m to a speculative buyer.

E. Heritage-Led Enabling Development
MODERATE FEASIBILITY

The Proposition

Use the enabling development route (NPPF 221 and NPPF 84(b)) to justify a small number of new dwellings in the grounds, where the revenue directly funds comprehensive restoration and long-term maintenance of the Grade II listed building.

Planning Route

  • Historic England's Enabling Development and the Conservation of Significant Places guidance
  • Must demonstrate: (a) the heritage asset needs significant investment; (b) market value alone cannot fund the work; (c) the development is the minimum necessary to secure the asset's future; (d) the public benefit outweighs the disbenefit
  • Independent conservation deficit assessment required
  • Open-book viability assessment
  • S106 to ring-fence enabling development proceeds for heritage restoration

The Case For

  • Grade II listed buildings of this size and age genuinely require substantial ongoing investment — oak structural maintenance, stained glass conservation, thatched/slate roofing
  • The guide price of £4.75m vs estimated value of £3.88m shows even at a reduced ask, the market premiums the acreage and potential above bricks-and-mortar value
  • Enabling development is an established, policy-backed route with Historic England support when genuine
  • Two or three high-quality dwellings tucked into the woodland could be almost invisible while generating £3–5m+ in revenue

Key Risks

  • The enabling development bar is high and the process is slow (12–18 months minimum)
  • Historic England may dispute the conservation deficit if the building is in reasonable condition
  • AONB + local opposition make the "minimum necessary" test very strict
  • All new dwellings subject to SALC H3 principal residence restriction

Indicative Value

If 2–3 dwellings achieved at £1.5–2.5m each, gross development value of £3–7.5m. But net proceeds after build costs, fees, and ring-fenced heritage spend would be significantly lower. This is a long-term play, not a quick profit.

◆ Recommended Approach
Phase 1: Low-Hanging Fruit (Scenarios A + B) Start with the outbuilding conversion/annexe and any extension works. These have clear precedent on Moult Hill, present the lowest planning risk, and add immediate value. The Cedar House annexe approval (3164/23/FUL) is your direct comparable. These works also build a positive relationship with the LPA and conservation officer.
Phase 2: The Paddock Play (Scenario D) The separate title (DN263903) is the real prize. 2.98 acres of freehold land on Moult Hill, likely outside the curtilage of the listing, with reduced heritage constraints. Pursue a single exceptional dwelling under NPPF §84(e) — Salcombe values justify the £500k+ architectural investment needed to clear the "truly outstanding" bar. Commission a heritage curtilage opinion and access/rights review early. If the paddock has independent access, it can be progressed in parallel with or independently of the main house works. Engage a design review panel-calibre architect (e.g., RIBA award winners with AONB experience).
Phase 3: Optimisation (Scenario C or E) If the paddock dwelling succeeds, consider whether the 8-bedroom main house would benefit from subdivision under NPPF §84(d), creating two more manageable principal-residence dwellings. This could be the long-term exit strategy: three dwellings across 7.2 acres on Moult Hill, each with principal residence S106, collectively worth £7–10m+.
Critical Pre-Acquisition Due Diligence
  • Curtilage opinion: DN263903 (2.98-acre paddock) is a separate title — obtain a heritage consultant's opinion on whether it falls within the curtilage of the listing. A separate title is strong evidence it does not, but not conclusive.
  • Access rights: Confirm whether DN263903 has independent highway access or relies on a right of way through DN170405. This determines whether the paddock can be sold/developed independently.
  • Conservation Area boundary: Confirm whether Moult Hill falls within the Salcombe Conservation Area
  • Pre-application advice: Pay for a formal pre-app with South Hams DC (including conservation officer and AONB officer) before exchanging contracts
  • TPO survey: Establish which trees are protected before assuming any development footprint
  • Bat survey: Commission a preliminary ecological appraisal and check whether the site falls within a South Hams SAC sustenance zone
  • Listed building condition survey: Quantify the conservation deficit — this supports both the enabling development route and the optimum viable use argument
